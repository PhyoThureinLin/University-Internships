Student Micro Internships


Part-time Work

Internships

Internships can be paid or unpaid. Both programs typically continue for one semester and allow you to apply what you are learning in the classroom to the job.

Consider of these practical experiences as a way to develop useful and applicable skills and create a distinctive portfolio of work experience that distinguishes you from your competitors.

Benefits of an Internship

  • Compared to students who either had an unpaid internship or no internship at all, those who had paid internships received over 50% more employment offers.
  • Professionalism, teamwork, communication, and problem-solving abilities are all enhanced through internships. The most significant characteristics of a position have been recognized as opportunities for skill development and amiable coworkers.
  • 70% of companies offer interns a full-time position, and 80% of students take them up on their offers.
Source: 2019 NACE Report

On-Campus Internships

The following University of Florida programs offer internships for credit.
UF Honors Program

For Honors students who have acquired an internship outside of their field of study or who are not eligible for internship credit through their department, internship credit (IDH4940) is available.

College of Liberal Arts of Sciences

Department of Political Science

Every undergraduate student in the political science department is encouraged to work as an intern in the executive, legislative, or judicial branches of government at the municipal, state, national, or international levels. Candidates for political office, political parties, interest groups (including nonprofit and lobbying organizations), and law firms involved in governmental relations and issue research all offer internship opportunities.

Department of Criminology and Sociology

Your undergraduate studies can be improved by participating in a practicum or internship (CCJ 4940), which gives students the chance to get useful real-world experience. You must be a criminology major in your third or fourth year to sign up for an internship.

Herbert Wertheim College of Engineering

Undergraduates are urged to apply for internships as a way to combine academic learning with real-world experience. Internships must be relevant to the academic major or desired career field and must be carried out under the supervision of an authorized business, institution, government agency, or industry. Blue-collar labor, technician job, data entry work, clerical work, part-time employment, etc. are not acceptable for internships.

College of Journalism and Communication

Department of Advertising

Two internship opportunities are available to students majoring in advertising through the department. While students with a more advanced internship opportunity may want to choose ADV 4941, the Advanced Advertising Internship, students wanting credit for a typical internship may like to consider ADV 4940, the Advertising Internship.

Department of Public Relations

Through PUR4940 - Public Relations Internship, students can get credit for their internship. For every 65 hours of internship work, students can earn one credit; for every 130 hours, they can earn two credits; and for every 195 hours, they can earn three credits. Up to three internship credits may be granted to students.
