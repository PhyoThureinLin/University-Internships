Part Time Work

Less time is required for part-time job than for full-time work, typically no more than 30 hours a week. The employer, however, is in charge of deciding what this maximum is. Part-time work can take on many different shapes and is a fantastic opportunity to obtain experience while still in school. Even if a part-time job is unrelated to your professional goals, these chances may nevertheless allow you to gain crucial transferrable skills that are necessary to succeed in the modern workforce.

Some types of part-time positions include:
  • Federal Work Study
  • Fellowships
  • Graduate Assistantships
  • On-campus Jobs
  • Positions with Local businesses or organizations

Benefits of Part Time Work

Dedicating 10-20 hours on or off campus affords you:
  • Several opportunities to earn money while learning
  • Several opportunities to earn money while learning
  • Investigate potential job paths
  • Obtain references and create compelling resume content.

Did you know?

According to the US Bureau of Labor Statistics, there are 5.5 million part-time workers in the US between the ages of 18 and 24.
