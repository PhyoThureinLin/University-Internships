What is a Micro Internship?

Micro internships are brief, project-based positions that are made available online by businesses. The length of a project, which typically ranges from 5 to 40 hours of work, might be anywhere from a week to a few months. Projects are intended to be finished all year long, and students are typically paid a set amount. Micro internships are designed to supplement current experiential learning and may be appealing to those who lack the resources or academic flexibility to relocate for a full-time internship. Additionally, micro internships give students access to practical experience while giving employers a scalable opportunity to engage with talent.

Through meaningful labor that benefits both the employer and the candidate, a micro internship connects students and alumni. Micro internships give students the chance to finish tasks while also assessing businesses for potential full-time employment prospects. Students can start establishing their professional networks by showcasing their abilities and work ethics. Benefits of Micro Internships

  • By completing projects for a variety of businesses, you can broaden your résumé.
  • Develop abilities and skills in a variety of areas to attract businesses.
  • Analyze businesses for potential future opportunities.
  • Build professional networks
  • Enhance academic performance and career preparation

How to apply for Micro Internships

The University of Florida connects students and employers to micro internship opportunities through the Parker Dewey* platform. Follow these easy steps to apply for a micro internship.

  • Click the link to the UF customized site
  • Create an account in less than five minutes
  • Complete your profile – Make sure to fill in all sections to best communicate your skills and abilities to prospective employers
  • Begin searching for projects

Additionally, you can search Gator CareerLink to find more internship and micro internship opportunities.
