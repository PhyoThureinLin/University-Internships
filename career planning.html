What is Career Planning?

Learning, discussing, and reflecting about your career are all considered to be part of career planning. The Career Connections Center views career planning as a continual process that aids in self-discovery, professional growth, experience accumulation, and preparation for subsequent steps. Our approach to career planning is centered on finding answers, learning new information, and acting.

How can Career Planning help you where you are today?

  • We consider you to be the expert. In order for you to live independently and responsibly about your job decisions, we assist you in identifying your potential.
  • We aid you in developing your personal and/or professional objectives, expanding your range of options.
  • We inquire appropriately and provide assignments and feedback to encourage reflection on your subsequent actions.

What are the ways you can engage in career planning?

At the Career Connection Center, to better meet your goals, we provide a range of alternative Career Planning solutions, including:

  • On your own with self-guided worksheets, guides, reflection journals, and tools/services
  • With Career Conversations, Workshops, and Labs as a group
  • Individually with Express Drop-In and Appointments for Career Planning
Here are some resources for career planning.

CHOMP - CHOMP is a self-directed, interactive, online method for planning your future education and profession that can assist you in making decisions.
Gator Professional Series - GPS is a series of online seminars for personal and professional development that go through the knowledge and abilities you need to be job-ready.
Career Communities - Access to career communities gives you access to information and resources that are specifically tailored to your college or other identities that may be significant to you.
Self-Assessments - Self-assessments can teach you more about yourself and give you further job suggestions.
